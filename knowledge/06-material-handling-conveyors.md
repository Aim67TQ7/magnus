# Material Handling & Conveyor Systems Technical Reference

## Overview

Bunting designs and builds custom magnetic and non-magnetic conveyor solutions for metal stamping, recycling, parts handling, and industrial material transport. This reference covers conveyor design principles, belt selection, feeding systems, and integration with magnetic separation equipment.

## General Design Principles

### Stainless Steel Rule (Critical)
Any conveyor frame or component near a magnetic element (pulleys, cross belts, rails, drums) **must be 300-series stainless steel** to prevent magnetization. Magnetized mild steel interferes with separation and causes material buildup. Extend stainless sections 1-2 feet minimum before and after the magnetic zone.

### Feeding Systems
Proper feeding is essential for magnetic separation effectiveness:
- **Vibratory feeders** — preferred for even, thin (monolayer) spread across belt/drum width
- **Side/end feeding conveyors** — generally poor for material spreading
- **Spreaders** — required on wider vibratory feeders
- **Khmer feeders** — poor for spreading material

### Key Principle
Magnetic separation efficiency depends on material being spread thin and uniform. Clumped or deep material creates bypass paths where contamination hides.

## Belt Selection Guide

| Belt Type | Application | Key Properties |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Bareback (low friction backing) | Slider bed conveyors, GFCs | Essential for reducing drag; never use rubber-backed |
| Monofilament | General use, horizontal transfers | Tracks well, less crown needed, 1.5:1 length:width ratio OK |
| PVC (e.g., Tuffy) | Standard applications | Open weave, harder to track, more crown needed |
| Urethane (E8/2U O U8) | Oily environments, stamping, scrap | Cut resistant, proprietary Bunting belt for punch presses |
| Rib Cleat / Cleat Top | Inclines with rolling parts | Cleat Top allows standard return rollers |
| Textured | Wet can applications | Prevents slippage on wet surfaces |

### Belt Tracking Rules
- Minimum length:width ratio: **3:1** for standard PVC belts, **1.5:1** for monofilament
- Crown pulleys: **1/8" per foot of width** for open weave, **0.020" per foot** for tight weave monofilament
- Larger pulleys make tracking easier
- Fine woven monofilament back belts track better than cheap open weave PVC

## Standard Frame Conveyor Design

### Specifications
- 6.5" frame conveyors
- 6" diameter end pulleys, 6.5" lagged drive pulleys
- Can be used as elevators (vertical, angled, horizontal)
- Can turn over parts

### Speed Rules
- **≤120 fpm**: Standard curved sections OK
- **>120 fpm**: Replace top curve with magnetic pulley (typically 18" diameter) to reduce frictional drag
- Corner pulleys range 6-24" diameter depending on speed and part size

### In-Feed Magnet Rules (Hopper Pull)
- In-feed magnet should be **weaker AND narrower** than main conveyor rail
- Too-strong magnet pulls up mass of parts from hopper
- Downsize one strength (e.g., 900T main → 600 in-feed)
- Make in-feed rail adjustable to pull away from slider bed
- Consider narrower rail (e.g., 3" wide BM 700 with two lanes of 1x1 magnets)

### Bath Rails (Decreasing Fields)
- Provide gradual step-down from full strength
- Never make decreasing field less than 8" long
- Only start decreasing field in curve if absolutely necessary

### Nails/Screws Handling
- Always use plate rail design inside conveyor
- Use rolled-over slider bed
- Nails/screws stick out from belt and attach to zap screws on standard slider bed
- They work inside frame and build up on rails over time

## Vibratory Feeder Integration with Magnetic Equipment

### Critical Requirements
1. **Feeder tray material**: MUST be 300-series stainless steel when near magnetic drums
2. **Width**: 2-3" narrower than drum face (prevents spillage)
3. **If ferrous tray discovered**: Add 11-gauge stainless skin extending 4-6" past feeder end

### Drum Flange Heights
| Application | Minimum Flange |
|-------------|---------------|
| Fines | 1" |
| Normal material | 2-3" |
| Large auto recycling | 4-6" |

### Design Considerations by Category
- **Mining (high density)**: Belt tensions 2-4x higher than biomass, heavy-duty steel framework, abrasion-resistant belting
- **Biomass (variable moisture)**: Seasonal moisture swings 20-30%, fire prevention critical, dust suppression required
- **Aggregate**: High abrasion, impact loading at transfer points

## Demagnetizer Integration

### When Required
- Parts must be demagnetized after magnetic handling
- Target: typically ≤10 gauss residual

### Installation Locations
- Discharge chutes of mag slides
- Horizontal sections of parts retrieval conveyors
- Horizontal sections of standard frame conveyors

### Considerations
- Light parts on horizontal belt may "float" in demagnetizing field — use cleats
- Very light parts on chute can get held up in field
- Consult Magnet Tool (Troy, Michigan) for sizing
- Specify: part size/weight, flow rate, target gauss level

## Eddy Current Separator Integration

### Purpose
Recovers non-ferrous metals (aluminum, copper, brass, zinc) AFTER ferrous removal.

### Operating Principle
Spinning magnets induce currents in non-ferrous metals, causing repulsion/ejection.

### Prerequisite
**Ferrous metal MUST be removed first** — ferrous particles damage the high-speed ECS rotor.

### Typical Staging
1. Overband (tramp protection)
2. Drum magnet (ferrous recovery)
3. Eddy Current Separator (non-ferrous recovery)

### Limitations
- Requires similar-sized particles for optimal separation
- Copper/aluminum separation is challenging with mixed sizes
- Density separators ("destoners") work better for rounder nuggets ≥3/8"

## Incinerated Scrap Separation Process
Tested multi-stage process for automotive incinerator output:
1. Vibratory feeder → horizontal conveyor under magnetic cross belt (easy ferrous)
2. Vibratory feeder → Neo deep reach drum (ferrous embedded in aluminum)
3. SSSC (12") for valves, springs, sheet metal, aluminum with ferrous
4. Eddy current for aluminum chunk separation

**Key insight**: Screening into size fractions (trommel preferred) before magnetic separation significantly improves all downstream equipment effectiveness:
- 1st fraction: 5/8" or 1/2" minus
- 2nd fraction: 1.5-2" minus
- 3rd fraction: 4-5" minus
- Overs: back to shredder

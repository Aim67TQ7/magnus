# Drum Magnet & Magnetic Head Pulley Technical Reference

## Overview

Drum magnets and magnetic head pulleys provide continuous ferrous metal separation at conveyor discharge points. They are critical for recycling/MRF ferrous recovery, product purity at discharge, and mineral processing applications.

## Equipment Types

### Drum Magnet (Standalone)
- Rotating shell around stationary magnetic element
- Material feeds onto drum surface; ferrous held and carried to underside discharge
- Non-magnetic material falls away at top
- Continuous, self-cleaning operation
- Best for: recycling ferrous recovery, mineral beneficiation, high-volume continuous separation

### Magnetic Head Pulley
- Replaces standard conveyor head pulley with magnetic pulley
- Material spreads thin at discharge — ferrous held to belt, non-ferrous falls freely
- Retrofit-friendly — installs in existing conveyor
- Best for: product purity at discharge, simple tramp protection, conveyor upgrades

### Wet Drum Separator
- Operates in slurry/liquid environment
- Used in mineral processing for magnetite recovery
- Available in LIMS, MIMS, HIMS configurations
- Best for: mining beneficiation, heavy media recovery

## Selection Framework

| Goal | Equipment | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| Equipment protection (upstream) | Overband (not drum) | Drum is at discharge, not upstream |
| Product purity at discharge | Magnetic Head Pulley | Material is thin/spread at discharge |
| Continuous ferrous recovery | Drum Magnet | Designed for high-volume recovery |
| Retrofit existing conveyor | Magnetic Head Pulley | Replaces standard pulley directly |
| Mineral beneficiation (wet) | Wet Drum Separator | Designed for slurry processing |
| Stainless/wire removal | HISC (not standard drum) | Standard drum cannot capture weakly magnetic |

## Magnetic Intensity Classifications

| Classification | Gauss Range | Captures | Application |
|----------------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| LIMS (Low Intensity) | <1,000-2,000 | Strongly magnetic (magnetite, large tramp) | First-line defense, magnetite recovery |
| MIMS (Medium Intensity) | 2,000-6,000 | Moderately magnetic minerals, finer contaminants | Reduces load before HIMS |
| HIMS (High Intensity) | 6,000-20,000+ | Weakly magnetic, paramagnetic (hematite, stainless) | Fine particle capture, mineral beneficiation |

**Key insight**: As intensity increases, you capture finer and less magnetic particles, but process design — not just raw gauss — determines real performance.

## Vibratory Feeder Integration (Critical)

When feeding a drum magnet from a vibratory feeder:

### Mandatory Rules
1. **Feeder tray MUST be 300-series stainless steel** — mild steel tray will magnetize even at 2-3" distance from drum
2. **Feeder width** should be 2-3" narrower than drum face to prevent spillage
3. **If feeder is mistakenly ferrous**, options:
   - Move feeder back or drum forward to increase gap
   - Add inside skin of 11-gauge stainless steel extending 4-6" past feeder end
   - Failure to correct → fines accumulate at feeder end, magnetize, create blockages

### Flange Heights
| Material Type | Minimum Flange Height |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Fines | 1" |
| Normal material | 2-3" |
| Large auto recycling | 4-6" |

## Sizing Considerations

### Drum Magnets
- Drum diameter determines separation zone length
- Drum width should match or exceed material stream width
- Speed affects separation — slower = better capture, lower throughput
- For materials >100 lb/ft³ density, specify heavy-duty construction

### Head Pulleys
- Must match existing conveyor pulley dimensions (diameter, face width, shaft)
- Magnetic arc coverage determines separation effectiveness
- Standard configurations: 180° and 270° magnetic arc

## Industry Applications

### Recycling / MRF
- Drum magnets after primary processing for continuous ferrous recovery
- Typical staging: Overband → Drum → Eddy Current (for non-ferrous)
- Self-cleaning designs required — contamination rates are high
- Armored/urethane belts for tire recycling and turf (wire puncture protection)

### Mining
- Wet drum separators for magnetite recovery
- LIMS for strongly magnetic minerals
- MIMS/HIMS for weakly magnetic mineral beneficiation
- Multiple stages common for increasing purity

### Food Processing
- Rare earth drum magnets for product purity
- Sanitary construction (304/316 SS)
- Positioned at discharge for final contamination capture

### Auto Shredder Residue
- High-capacity drum for ferrous recovery
- Followed by SSSC for stainless, then eddy current for aluminum
- Liberation (proper shredding) is critical to separation effectiveness
- Screening into size fractions significantly improves all downstream separation

# Drawer & Grate Magnet Technical Reference

## Overview

Drawer and grate magnets are used in gravity-fed (free-fall) applications to capture ferrous contaminants from powders, granules, pellets, and other dry bulk materials. They are the primary solution for food, pharmaceutical, plastics, and chemical processing where material flows through chutes, hoppers, or feed throats.

## Equipment Types

### Grate Magnets
- Rows of magnetic tubes/bars in a housing
- Material flows through gaps between tubes
- Simple, cost-effective, easy to clean manually
- Best for: free-flowing granules, pellets, low-contamination environments

### Drawer Magnets (Standard)
- Magnetic grate mounted in a pull-out drawer housing
- Easy-clean design — slide drawer out, wipe tubes
- Available in round and square housings
- Best for: production environments requiring frequent cleaning

### HF Drawer Magnet (High Flux)
- Enhanced magnetic circuit for maximum gauss on tube surface
- 10,000+ gauss standard
- Best for: food CCP (Critical Control Point), pharmaceutical, high-purity requirements

### FF Drawer Magnet (Free Flow)
- Designed for maximum flow capacity with minimal restriction
- Wider tube spacing reduces bridging
- Best for: high-throughput applications, materials prone to bridging

### TurboGrate (Rotary)
- Motorized rotating magnetic tubes
- Breaks bridges, prevents material hang-up
- Self-cleaning action on rotation cycle
- Best for: sticky/cohesive powders (flour, cocoa, starch, spices)

## Selection Decision Framework

| Material Behavior | Recommended Equipment | Reason |
|-------------------|----------------------|--------|
| Dry free-flowing (sugar, pellets, granules) | Standard grate or drawer | Simple, effective, low maintenance |
| Sticky/bridging (flour, cocoa, starch) | TurboGrate (rotary) | Spinning breaks lumps, prevents bridging |
| Abrasive (silica, glass, mineral) | Plate magnet or hump magnet | Wear-resistant construction required |
| Food CCP / pharma | HF Drawer (high flux) | Maximum gauss, sanitary design, documentation |
| High throughput | FF Drawer (free flow) | Wider spacing, reduced restriction |
| Plastic pellets/regrind | Standard drawer or grate | Position above extruder feed throat |

### CRITICAL RULE
**If powder bridges or rat-holes → NEVER use static grate.** You need mechanical agitation (TurboGrate/rotary) or larger cross-section housing.

## Specifications

### Magnetic Performance
- **Standard ceramic**: 2,500-4,000 gauss on tube surface
- **Rare earth (NdFeB)**: 10,000-12,000+ gauss on tube surface
- **Food/pharma minimum**: 10,000 gauss (rare earth required)

### Construction
- **Food grade**: 304 or 316 stainless steel, sanitary welds, no crevices
- **Industrial**: 304 stainless steel standard
- **Certifications available**: USDA, FDA, HACCP, FSMA, 3-A (dairy)

### Temperature Limits
- **Standard NdFeB**: 176°F (80°C) maximum
- **High-temp NdFeB**: Up to 350°F (175°C)
- **Samarium Cobalt (SmCo)**: Up to 570°F (300°C)
- **Ceramic**: Up to 450°F (230°C)

## Installation Best Practices

### Positioning
- Mount directly above equipment being protected (extruder feed throat, hopper inlet, conveyor transfer)
- Minimize distance between magnet and material entry point
- Ensure full material stream passes through magnetic field — no bypass paths

### Feeding
- Material must flow evenly across full cross-section of magnet tubes
- Use vibratory feeder or spreader to distribute material uniformly
- Uneven flow creates bypass paths where contamination escapes

### Cleaning Frequency
- **Food CCP**: Every shift or more frequently (document every cleaning)
- **Food PCP**: Daily minimum
- **Industrial**: When buildup approaches saturation
- **Key rule**: If buildup approaches saturation, efficiency is already degraded

## Industry-Specific Requirements

### Food Processing
- Rare earth (10,000+ gauss) required for food safety
- Sanitary 304 or 316 SS construction
- Easy-clean or self-cleaning design
- HACCP/FSMA documentation
- CCP magnets require validated cleaning procedures

### Plastics / Extrusion
- Position above extruder feed throat
- Protects expensive screws and barrels
- High-intensity for fine metal capture
- Consider metal detector downstream for verification
- **NEVER use gravity-fed grates for plastic film** — static causes clogging; use drag slide

### Pharmaceutical
- 316 SS construction, sanitary welds
- 3-A certification may be required for dairy
- Full validation documentation
- Gauss testing records required
